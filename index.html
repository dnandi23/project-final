Medical Solutions

Solutions for Flatfoot

  • Custom orthotic insoles to provide better arch support.
  • Physical therapy exercises to strengthen the foot muscles:-
    1. Heel cord stretches:involves gently lengthening the Achilles tendon to improve flexibility and alleviate pain or tightness in the calf and heel.
    2. Arch lifts: trengthen the intrinsic muscles in the foot, particularly those supporting the arch, by lifting the arch while keeping the toes and heel on the ground.
    3. Towel curls: It is done in a seating position with a towel under your involved foot while seated draw the towel back towards you using your toes. It is an effective and easy way to strengthen the muscles in your feet, particularly the plantar fascia
    4. Tennis/golf ball rolls: Rolling a tennis or golf ball under the foot can help to massage and stretch the plantar fascia.
    5. Toe raises:strengthens the muscles on the front of your lower leg (shin muscles) and improves ankle stability and balance.
  • Proper footwear with good arch support and cushioning.
  • Surgical options for severe cases if non-invasive treatments are ineffective.

When to seek Medical assistance

  • If you experience persistent pain, stiffness, or difficulty walking.
  • If you suspect you have flat feet and are experiencing symptoms.
  • If non-surgical treatments are not providing adequate relief.
  • If you have a history of foot problems or injuries.

Solutions for High Arch

  • Use of cushioning insoles to reduce foot strain and absorb shock.
  • Stretching exercises to prevent tightness in the foot muscles.
  • Wearing shoes with adequate arch support and cushioning.
  • Physical therapy to improve foot function and balance.
    1. Balance Exercises:Exercises that improve proprioception (awareness of body position) like standing on one foot or using a wobble board can improve balance and stability.
    2. Ankle Muscles: Exercises like ankle inversions and eversions strengthen the muscles around the ankle joint, improving stability and reducing the risk of sprains.
    3. Arch Support:Exercises like arch lifts (foot doming) can strengthen the muscles that support the arch.
    4. Heel Cord StretchStretching the Achilles tendon and calf muscles can help reduce tension that can pull on the plantar fascia.

Solutions for Normal Foot

  • Maintain a healthy weight to avoid excessive pressure on the feet.
  • Regular foot stretches and exercises to keep the muscles flexible.
  • Wear shoes that support the natural arch of the foot.
  • Regular check-ups with a podiatrist to monitor foot health.
